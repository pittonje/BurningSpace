# New Player Onboarding

## Goal

Onboarding should teach the player the account loop, safe resource collection, first ship purchase, basic combat, and the first hired pilot without overwhelming them with the full strategic game.

## Proposed First Cycle

1. The player creates an account.
2. The player chooses a faction, or receives a temporary faction assignment.
3. The hangar contains one starter ship: a small asteroid miner.
4. The Miner is weak, slow, and nearly useless in PvP.
5. A safe starter asteroid zone exists near the allied base.
6. The tutorial directs the player to mine resources.
7. The player buys the first Scout-class combat ship.
8. Scout properties:
   - faster than other base classes;
   - lower HP and weaker weapons;
   - intended for scouting, interception, and learning combat.
9. The player travels toward the frontline.
10. Destroying enemy NPCs and helping in combat grants more resources than safe mining.
11. After buying a second ship, the player unlocks the first hired NPC pilot.
12. The pilot is assigned to the unused Miner.
13. The player selects mining behavior for that pilot.
14. The pilot travels to mine resources.
15. If the owner is absent for more than five minutes:
   - the pilot stops the active order;
   - returns to an allied base;
   - stops producing resources after returning.
16. The tutorial ends.
17. The Fighter class unlocks.
18. The Shipyard offers Fighter hulls.
19. The player starts collecting resources for the first Fighter.
20. A later class slot can unlock, with Support as the working name.
21. Additional pilots can be hired later.
22. The player forms a wing from one personally controlled ship and NPC-controlled ships.

## Intended Learning Sequence

- Account and hangar are persistent.
- Ships are owned instances, not temporary loadouts.
- Safe mining is slow but reliable.
- Frontline participation is riskier and more rewarding.
- Pilots extend the player's activity, but do not become unlimited offline farming.
- A wing is a tactical group, not the entire hangar.

## Open Questions

- Can the tutorial mining zone be attacked?
- Can a player change faction after onboarding?
- Are ships permanently lost when destroyed?
- Is repair paid, timed, free, or insurance-based?
- Can a hired NPC pilot die permanently?
- Is cargo lost on death?
- How is safe AFK farming prevented?
- Can the player personally control any ship in the active wing during a sortie?
- Can the player switch between ships during combat?
- Is faction choice made before or after the tutorial?
- What happens if the player disconnects during the tutorial?
